    About

    Hello and thanks for checking out our project!

    Lancaster City Museum is located in the former town hall in the city centre of Lancaster, North West of England. The museum houses a mixture between temporary and permanent exhibitions. The Core exhibitions of the museum are Lancaster Roman Tombstone, and the King’s Own Regiment.

    In fact, Lancaster City Museum holds the biggest catalogue of King’s Own regiment memorabilia with over thirty thousand items in the collection - dating back to 921AD! (King's Own Royal Regiment Museum Lancaster, 2016) While, the temporary exhibitions are normally produced by in-house or local artists that changes every few months.

    The growth of the museum is stagnated and is unable to attract more visitors each year. 

    Our team has identified couple of issues that needs to be addressed in order to boost the performance of the museum.

    1. In spite of representing the city’s rich history, the museum is unable to make its presence known to the community and does not effectively communicate its unique selling point of representing Lancaster’s history and having special exhibits to the public
    2. The museum does not actively engage with the community. Currently, only a few local schools make sporadic visits to the museum. Further, the museum’s last collaboration with a local organisation was already 4 years ago with Lancaster University's Chinese Society.
    3. Lancaster City Museum fail to create interactions, as majority of their exhibitions are simply showing artifacts, posters, and texts. The only interactions are the Train model in the upper floor and quiz sheets given to children.

    What are we doing about it?

    We are looking to implement Augmented Reality (AR) in Lancaster City museum to create a more immersive and engaging experience. By digitalising the exhibits in the museum, we can preserve the history of the city, and make it more accessible to the general public. The current and next generations could then access more easily the rich history of the city.

     Immerse yourself into the local heritage of our community. Let's showcase Lancaster's heritage for generations to come.

    With our project, the local history can be reserved and become accessible for many generations to come. Our project also aims to engage with the community and benefit younger generations. With this campaign, we try to bring the community together to protect and conserve the history of this community.

    After the implementation of AR to the museum, we will partner up with local school and council to create educational curriculums for local children. The AR exhibitions will provide an interactive way for children to learn the local history. By having more enjoyment in learning, we can cultivate children’s their interest in learning, the local history in particular (Ainley and Ainley, 2010), and hopefully develop a high sense of belonging to the local community that they will protect in the future.

    Help us make this happen!

    We are looking to raise £28,000 in order to bring this ambitious project to life. All proceeds are going to be invested in the development costs for the AR app and for the 3D scanning of the artifacts, allowing us to preserve and access Lancaster's rich history at our fingertips.
